Robot Design

Designed by Ziming

Mistakes and Lessons Learned

With Creative Cats meeting only once a week and not all members being able to attend every session, we faced tight timelines that delayed full completion of our intake mechanism by LM3 despite solid prototypes in LM2. This delayed coding and intake practice sessions, as both depended on a finished intake mechanism.

Robot Overview

Modularity

We design every major subsystem as a module so we can remove four to eight screws and swap or repair that system without touching the rest of the robot.

V1
  • Used Duo's standard channel rails
  • Mounted intake and launcher separately
  • Allowed for quick removal during early tests
V2
  • Allowed motors, ramps, and tubes to be dismantled
  • Enhanced modularity for easier repairs

Reliability and Low-Maintenance

We built Creative Cats' robot on REV's proven DUO FTC Starter Bot platform, using its robust channel drivetrain, double-supported shafts, and reliable intake.

V1
  • Survived over 20 matches with no breakage
  • Very minimal repair work/maintenance
  • Allowed improvement instead of rebuilding
V2
  • Built to last 200 rounds
  • Included walls and better balance
  • Enhanced durability and stability

Driver-Centered Design

All scoring actions are mapped to single buttons rather than combos, so the driver can focus on lining up rather than remembering sequences. Everything is tuned for the driver's ease of use.

V1
  • Clear sight lines and intuitive controls
  • Drivers can score under pressure without frustration
V2
  • Planned everything around how our driver operates
  • Simple controls for focus on scoring and field awareness

Clean Serviceability

We prioritize serviceability because we can swap motors or fix issues fast in the pit. Easy access lets us fix issues in minutes during comps, keeping us competitive even after damage.

V1
  • Emphasizes accessible wiring
  • Centered battery placement
  • Modular channels
V2
  • Implements reliability and easy repair
  • Enhanced serviceability features

Initial Design: REV Starter Bot

Our first robot design, which we used for LM1, was the REV Starter Bot. This bot used a plastic ramp to hold multiple artifacts, as well as a flywheel to launch them into the bin. While this design was a great starting point, there were definitely some flaws that we were looking to improve. Specifically, we wanted to improve the accuracy of our shooting and the efficiency of our robot.

Software Improvements

We made drastic software changes including a fully working 3-ball autonomous routine, plus tweaks to motor power levels and servo directional control for more precise artifact handling and scoring. These updates transformed our LM3 robot's performance, giving us a higher average score per round.

Final Robot Features

Our final robot features a fully functioning 3-ball intake powered by our chain-link conveyor system for reliable multi-artifact pickup. It also runs a consistent 9-ball autonomous routine that navigates, shoots, and scores with high accuracy. This drastically improved our bot's capabilities and raised our average score considerably.
